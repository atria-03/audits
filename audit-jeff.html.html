Where Infinity is leaking — at franchise scale
High volume means more missed calls, not fewer
At 300+ monthly inquiries, even a 25% miss rate is 75 people per month who never heard back. Each one is a procedure that went to a competitor. The bigger the practice, the more expensive this problem becomes — and it scales with every new franchise location.
Membership and waitlist management is a manual load
Infinity's membership and treatment programme model means staff are managing active member check-ins, waitlist placements, and renewals manually. At franchise scale, this breaks down and becomes a retention leak across every location.
After-hours leads go cold before the next business day
A high-review business like Infinity generates evening and weekend inquiries constantly — people researching after work. Those leads sit in a queue until Monday morning. Competitors with instant response win those patients before you see the notification.
Franchise rollout multiplies every gap across locations
Every operational gap at the original location gets replicated at each franchise site. Inconsistent inquiry response, missed after-hours leads, and manual follow-up don't just cost revenue — they risk the consistency that makes a franchise model work.
